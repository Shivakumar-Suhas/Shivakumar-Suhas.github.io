## Overview
This work focuses on the **structural design and optimization of a CFRP wing box for a fixed-wing eVTOL aircraft**, targeting **weight-efficient load-carrying capability** under representative flight conditions. The study integrates **load analysis, laminate design, and finite element validation** to develop a structurally efficient composite configuration.

## Structural Modeling & Load Analysis
- Developed an **FE model of the wing–fuselage system** to capture global load transfer behavior.  
- Extracted **load paths and sectional resultants** including **bending moments, shear forces, and torsional loads** under representative **flight and maneuver load cases**.  
- Identified critical regions for structural sizing within the **wing box (spar caps, skins, and shear webs)**.

## Laminate Design & Optimization
- Designed CFRP laminates using **Classical Laminate Theory (CLT)** combined with **ply-level FE analysis**.  
- Applied **stiffness-driven sizing** to meet deflection and load-bearing requirements.  
- Developed **balanced and symmetric stacking sequences** ensuring manufacturability and structural efficiency.  
- Iteratively optimized ply orientations (**0°, ±45°, 90°**) for combined **bending–torsion loading**.

## Analyses
- **Static structural analysis** to evaluate stress distribution and deformation behavior.  
- **Buckling analysis** of wing box panels and shear webs under compressive loading.  
- Verification of laminate performance under combined loading using **failure criteria (e.g., Hashin / maximum stress)**.

## Results & Key Outcomes
- Achieved a **weight-efficient CFRP wing box design** meeting structural stiffness and strength requirements.  
- Optimized laminates provided improved **load distribution and torsional stiffness** under coupled loading conditions.  
- Buckling constraints governed sizing in critical regions, ensuring **structural stability of thin-walled sections**.

## Notes
- The study assumes **idealized load cases and boundary conditions** representative of preliminary design stages.  
- Future work can include **aeroelastic coupling, fatigue assessment, and detailed joint design (fasteners/co-cured interfaces)**.  
- Manufacturing considerations such as **ply draping and thickness transitions** can be further refined in detailed design stages.
